Glossary of Terms
Not sure what a term means? Every technical term in this report is explained in plain English below.
Citation
Any online listing that mentions your business Name, Address, and Phone number — used by Google to verify your location and build trust in your business.
CPA (Cost Per Acquisition)
How much it costs to gain one paying customer from ads — total ad spend divided by new customers gained.
CPC (Cost Per Click)
How much you pay each time someone clicks your Google Ad. For Winnipeg locksmiths, this is approximately CAD $8–$14 per click for emergency keywords.
CPL (Cost Per Lead)
Total ad spend divided by the number of leads (calls or form fills) you received. Noble's projected CPL is CAD $71–$92 per lead.
CTR (Click-Through Rate)
The percentage of people who saw your listing or ad and clicked on it. Map Pack position #1 gets 42% CTR vs 3% for businesses not in the top 3.
GA4 (Google Analytics 4)
Google's website analytics platform that tracks who visits your site, where they came from, and what they do — including how many people call you from your website.
GBP (Google Business Profile)
Your free Google listing that appears in Maps and local search results. This is what shows Noble Locksmith's name, address, phone number, hours, reviews, and photos when someone searches for a locksmith.
GTM (Google Tag Manager)
A tool that lets tracking codes be added to your website without editing the site code directly — used to set up call tracking and conversion measurement.
LSA (Local Services Ads)
Google ads that show above regular search ads and charge per lead (phone call), not per click. For locksmiths in Canada, typical cost is CAD $40–$70 per booked call.
LTV (Lifetime Value)
The total revenue a single customer is expected to generate over their relationship with Noble — including repeat jobs, referrals, and commercial contracts. Estimated at 5× the first job value.
Map Pack
The box of 3 local business listings shown at the top of Google search results when someone searches "locksmith Winnipeg." Being in the top 3 is the single biggest driver of inbound calls.
NAP (Name, Address, Phone)
The three pieces of business contact information that must be identical everywhere online. Any mismatch hurts Noble's Google ranking.
Review Velocity
The average number of new reviews Noble Locksmith receives per month. Currently ~2.4/month. Target is 10–15/month to enter and hold the Map Pack.
ROAS (Return on Ad Spend)
Revenue earned for every dollar spent on ads. A 4× ROAS means CAD $4 back for every CAD $1 spent on Google Ads.
RSA (Responsive Search Ad)
A Google ad format where multiple headlines and descriptions are provided, and Google automatically tests the best combinations to maximize clicks and calls.
Schema Markup
Hidden code added to Noble's website that helps Google understand the business name, address, phone, hours, and services — improving how Noble appears in search results.
